Think in March

Posted on 2025-03-28 Edited on 2025-03-29 In research

Today, I suddenly realized that my mindset needs to change. As a graduate student, I should no longer see myself just as an undergraduate or technician. More specifically, I need to take my seriously and stop treating it like a toy and game. Research requires a professional attitude and I should set higher standards for my self.

Moreover, I need to move beyond the mindset of student. Graduate studies more like a job, not only the format, but also the content. I must learn how to answer questions clearly, deliver compelling presentations and communicate efficiently with my advisor.Preparing well for the individuall meetings is essential. Additionally, honesty is key. When I encounter problems, I should acknowledge them, keep them in minds and work step by step to refine my approach.

Spend more time on preparation and regularly summarizing my progress will also be beneficial.Writing is one of the best way to organize thoughts and express ideas with precision. It can also help me improve my spoken english by reinforcing key concepts.

At this stage, I can still learn a lot from my advisor.
Too often I find myself caught up in trivial technical details and lose sight of the bigger picture. To stay focused, I should structure my thinking around the following questions:

  1. What problem am I trying to solve? (objective)
  2. Why is this worth addressing? (motivation)
  3. what knowledge or data do I already have? (data)
  4. How do you use the knowledge or date I have to tackle this problem? (method)
  5. How about the result? compare with existing work? (results)

I hope that in my future research whether reading papers or giving presentation, I can consistently follow this framework. It is easy to say these things but applying them in practice is the real challenge.

Additionally, when reading papers, I should focus on identifying the key differences and innovation compared to previous work. Recognizing what sets a paper apart is crucial for deep understanding.

Last but not least, keep in mind the physical meaning is the basical. In any physical model, it should align with the fundamental physical principle. Ensuring this correspondence is essential for meaningful research.

And when I encouter problems or have unclear definations. I should dedicate my efforts to thoroughly and deeply overcome these issues.

Introduction

Camera is one of the most powerful tools in computer vision (CV), machine vision (MV) and computational imaging (CI). We use this mechanism to recording rhe world around us and get the photographs for various application.
In the research field of CV,MV,CI, how can we model the camera is an essential problem.

Pinhole cameras

The pinhole camera model is the simplest camera model which just consists of a pinhole instead of a lens. And the 3D coordinates are projected onto a 2D image plane through the pinhole.

Each point on the 3D object emits multiple rays of light outwards. With barrier, only one or a few of these rays passes through the aperture and hits the film.
